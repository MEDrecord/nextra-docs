## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards and behavioral expectations for all MEDrecord personnel to ensure integrity, professionalism, and information security.

## Scope

This code applies to all MEDrecord employees, contractors, interns, and anyone acting on behalf of MEDrecord.

## Core Values

### Integrity
Act honestly and ethically in all business dealings.

### Respect
Treat colleagues, customers, and partners with dignity and respect.

### Excellence
Strive for quality and continuous improvement in all work.

### Security
Protect information and systems as a core responsibility.

### Compliance
Adhere to laws, regulations, and company policies.

## Expected Behaviors

### Professional Conduct

**Do:**
- Perform duties with diligence and professionalism
- Communicate respectfully and constructively
- Support a collaborative work environment
- Report concerns through appropriate channels
- Represent MEDrecord positively

**Do Not:**
- Engage in harassment or discrimination
- Use intimidation or bullying
- Make false statements or misrepresentations
- Engage in conflicts of interest
- Violate confidentiality agreements

### Information Security

**Do:**
- Protect confidential and sensitive information
- Use strong passwords and enable MFA
- Lock screens when away from workstations
- Report suspicious activities immediately
- Follow data classification guidelines

**Do Not:**
- Share credentials with anyone
- Access information without authorization
- Bypass security controls
- Install unauthorized software
- Use company systems for personal gain

### Healthcare Data (Patient Information)

As a healthcare technology company, we have special responsibilities:

**Do:**
- Access patient data only when necessary for your role
- Handle healthcare information per NEN 7510
- Maintain patient confidentiality always
- Report potential HIPAA/AVG violations
- Complete healthcare privacy training

**Do Not:**
- Access patient records out of curiosity
- Discuss patient information inappropriately
- Share patient data without authorization
- Store patient data on personal devices
- Take photos of patient information

### Use of Company Resources

**Do:**
- Use resources primarily for business purposes
- Protect company property and assets
- Return equipment upon termination
- Report lost or stolen equipment immediately

**Do Not:**
- Use resources for illegal activities
- Excessive personal use of company systems
- Copy proprietary information for personal use
- Damage or misuse company property

### Conflicts of Interest

**Disclose:**
- Financial interests in competitors or vendors
- Family relationships with business partners
- Outside employment or consulting
- Gifts or entertainment over €50

**Avoid:**
- Personal relationships affecting judgment
- Using company information for personal gain
- Competing with MEDrecord
- Accepting bribes or kickbacks

### Anti-Corruption

MEDrecord prohibits:
- Bribery in any form
- Facilitation payments
- Improper gifts or entertainment
- Money laundering
- Fraud

### Intellectual Property

**Protect:**
- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Source code and technical documentation
- Customer lists and business strategies
- Research and development information

**Respect:**
- Third-party intellectual property rights
- Software licenses and copyrights
- Patents and trademarks
- Confidential information of others

### External Communication

**Do:**
- Direct media inquiries to Communications
- Get approval before public speaking
- Use social media responsibly
- Clearly separate personal views from company positions

**Do Not:**
- Speak on behalf of MEDrecord without authorization
- Share confidential information externally
- Make false claims about products or services
- Disparage competitors unethically

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

- Direct manager (first option)
- HR department
- Security Officer
- Compliance hotline (anonymous option)
- Legal department

### Non-Retaliation

MEDrecord prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports violations in good faith
- Participates in investigations
- Refuses to participate in violations

### Investigation Process

1. Report received and logged
2. Initial assessment
3. Investigation (if warranted)
4. Findings documented
5. Appropriate action taken
6. Reporter notified (where possible)

## Consequences

Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Required training
- Suspension
- Termination
- Legal action
- Regulatory reporting

Severity depends on:
- Nature of violation
- Intent
- Prior violations
- Impact on company/others

## Acknowledgment

All personnel must:
- Read and understand this Code
- Sign acknowledgment upon joining
- Complete annual acknowledgment
- Report any violations
